Fellowship Cohort

Get your teaching staff recognised. A structured, cohort-based program that supports academics toward HEA Fellowship — through writing support, peer review and mentoring — and lifts your school's teaching profile in the process.

The four categories

AFHEA
Associate Fellow
Early-career, sessional and those supporting learning — evidence a focused slice of practice.
FHEA
Fellow
Established teachers with a substantive, rounded teaching role.
SFHEA
Senior Fellow
Those who influence and support the practice of others.
PFHEA
Principal Fellow
Strategic leaders of learning and teaching across an institution.

How the cohort works

01 · MAP
Find the right category
Each participant is matched to the Fellowship category that fits their role and evidence.
02 · WRITE
Guided writing sprints
Structured sessions that turn everyday teaching into framework-aligned evidence.
03 · REVIEW
Peer review & mentoring
Cohort peer review plus mentor feedback before submission.
04 · SUBMIT
Ready to submit
Participants leave with a complete, confident application.

What's included

  • Category-matching for every participant
  • A facilitated writing-sprint series
  • Framework-aligned templates and exemplars
  • Structured peer review within the cohort
  • Mentor feedback before submission
  • Links to the relevant crash courses for evidence
  • A school-level recognition snapshot (who's ready next)
  • Repeatable each intake

Independent & honest. NTLSN supports staff toward recognition; HEA Fellowship is awarded by Advance HE against its Professional Standards Framework, through your institution's accredited scheme or Advance HE's direct route — NTLSN is independent of Advance HE and does not award Fellowship or guarantee an outcome.
