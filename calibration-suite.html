Calibration Suite

The fairest thing you can do for students is make sure a 72 from one marker is a 72 from every marker. The Calibration Suite runs a simple ritual before marking starts: everyone scores the same sample, you see the spread, and you talk until it closes. Try the live spread checker below.

The calibration ritual

01 · SAMPLE
Pick a shared script
Choose 2–3 anonymised student samples that span the grade range.
02 · SCORE BLIND
Everyone marks alone
Each marker scores the same sample independently against the rubric — no conferring yet.
03 · SEE THE SPREAD
Reveal together
Plot every marker's score. The gap between highest and lowest is the number that matters.
04 · CLOSE THE GAP
Talk & align
Discuss the outliers, agree what the rubric means, re-mark. Repeat until tight.

Why calibrate?

Fairness you can defend
If a grade appeal lands, "we calibrated before marking" is the answer moderators want to hear.
Rubrics that actually mean something
The disagreements surface exactly which criteria are read differently — then you fix the wording.
Sessional onboarding, fast
New and casual markers get inside the standard in one session instead of a semester.
Evidence for recognition
Leading calibration is assessment leadership — strong SFHEA and award evidence.
