Alt-text helper.

Good alt text makes your slides, PDFs and LMS pages work for screen-reader users — and it's quick once you know the role the image plays. Pick the role, follow the guidance, draft, check the length. Nothing is stored or sent.

The golden rule: describe the image's purpose in context, not every pixel. The same photo needs different alt text in a biology lecture vs a design class. Don't start with "Image of…" — screen readers already announce it's an image.
