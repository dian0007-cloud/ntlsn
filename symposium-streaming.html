What it does pilot

  • One connection, your account. Uses your university's own Zoom via Zoom's official user-managed OAuth — NTLSN never owns your meetings or data.
  • Registration on your page. A registration form on NTLSN pushes attendees straight into your Zoom (each gets their unique join link).
  • Watch on NTLSN. The live symposium embeds on your event page via Zoom's Meeting SDK — attendees never leave the commons.
  • Automatic replay. When Zoom finishes the cloud recording, the replay link publishes itself to the event page.
  • Sign in with ORCID. Researcher identity, free and standard — no new password.

Honest status — what's real today

  • Built & live: the interface, the Sign-in-with-ORCID flow, and the serverless backend stubs (Zoom OAuth connect, webhook verifier, Meeting-SDK signature) — on NTLSN's existing infrastructure.
  • Rolling out with founding universities: live cross-institution connection needs a Zoom Marketplace app review (so other universities can authorize their Zoom) plus a secure token store. That's the pilot work, not a basic-research claim.
  • Your IT keeps control: least-privilege scopes, account-wide admin consent, one-click disconnect (we delete your data), HMAC-verified webhooks. Nothing is hidden.
